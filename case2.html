COUNTERPARTY RISK · CAPITAL OPTIMIZATION

The credit line said "No." The portfolio structure said otherwise.

A counterparty’s exposure had hit the terminal appetite—the hard ceiling where risk appetite and revenue potential collide. Expanding the line was a non-starter, yet multiple desks saw high-quality, lucrative opportunities left on the table.

3 min read
OUTCOME

$150M in additional trading capacity unlocked by recognizing cross-product netting benefits—allowing business growth without increasing the net risk footprint.

The framework and methodology below are distilled from this experience. All figures are illustrative of the structural approach used to resolve the capacity bottleneck.

01. THE CONTEXT

Inefficient capital allocation starts with fragmented data.

One client, three business lines, three disconnected legal wrappers. The risk system viewed these as independent risks, failing to account for the natural hedges between them.

Business Line Legal Document Gross Exposure Margin Held Net Risk (Siloed)
Synthetics Prime Brokerage $400M $50M $350M
Stockloan GMSLA $200M $30M $170M
OTC Derivatives ISDA $300M $40M $260M
TOTAL — $900M $120M $780M (At Cap)

Illustrative data representing the "Pre-MNET" state where offsets are ignored.

02. THE PROBLEM

When Risk-Off blocks Alpha-On.

The Cap: Credit Risk had set a hard limit at $780M.

The Constraint: The Synthetics and OTC desks wanted to increase exposure by $150M to capture market volatility.

The Reality: Under the current siloed calculation, any new trade would breach the terminal appetite. However, the client's Stockloan positions were often directional hedges against their Synthetics portfolio. We were holding capital against risk that didn't economically exist.

03. THE SOLUTION

Integrating legal and mathematical risk into a single lens.

The solution wasn't to ask for more risk—it was to measure the risk more accurately.

Structural Breakdown:

Deconstruct the Prime Brokerage and OTC business lines to identify overlapping risk factors. This required a deep dive into the underlying collateral and sensitivity (Greeks) across all three desks.

The MNET Overlay:

Conceptualize a Master Netting (MNET) Agreement. This is a legal "bridge" that allows the Firm to net obligations across different legal documents (ISDA, GMSLA, PB) in a default scenario.

Cross-Functional Execution:

Legal: Drafted the overlay to ensure enforceability across jurisdictions.

Model Risk: Validated that the PFE (Potential Future Exposure) models could handle cross-product correlations.

Credit Risk: Shifted the monitoring from "Sum of Silos" to a "Portfolio-Level" view.

04. THE ASSUMPTIONS

Where it Breaks

Legal Enforceability Risk

If the MNET overlay isn't legally robust in the client’s home jurisdiction, a liquidator could "cherry-pick"—demanding payment on winning trades while defaulting on losing ones. Netting only works if it's legally bulletproof.

Correlation Breakdown

Netting assumes that if one position loses value, another (the hedge) will gain. In extreme market stress, correlations can break. Your "offset" might vanish exactly when you need it most.

Liquidity Mismatches

Netting a highly liquid Stockloan position against an illiquid OTC derivative is risky. If you can't exit the hedge at the same speed as the primary exposure, the "netted" view is an illusion.

Operational Complexity

Consolidating margin across three different desks requires sophisticated collateral management systems. If your operations team can't track the unified margin call, you risk being under-collateralized.

REPLICABLE DIAGNOSTIC

Use structural innovation to bypass artificial limits.

When a credit line is full, don't ask for a higher limit—look for Unrecognized Netting.

The Test: Map your top 10 counterparties. For those at their limit, calculate the "Gross-to-Net" ratio across all legal documents. If the ratio is high, you have a documentation problem, not a risk problem.

Risk Management isn't just about saying "No"; it's about building the infrastructure that allows the business to say "Yes" safely.
